Fluids

What are Fluids Dynamics & Mechanics?

Fluid mechanics and dynamics study the behavior of fluids (liquids and gases) at rest and in motion. Fluid mechanics covers properties like density and viscosity, and hydrostatics, which analyzes pressure and forces in stationary fluids. Dynamics focuses on fluid motion, including flow in pipes, open channels, and around objects, driven by forces, energy, and momentum. Key applications include designing pipelines, aircraft, and hydraulic systems, ensuring efficient and safe fluid transport.

1. Fluid Properties

Density: \( \rho = \frac{m}{V} \)

Specific Weight: \( \gamma = \rho g \)

Specific Gravity: \( SG = \frac{\rho}{\rho_{\text{water}}} \)

Viscosity (Dynamic): \( \mu = \tau \frac{dy}{du} \)

Kinematic Viscosity: \( \nu = \frac{\mu}{\rho} \)

Surface Tension: \( \sigma = \frac{F}{L} \)

Pressure: \( P = \frac{F}{A} \)

2. Hydrostatics

Hydrostatic Pressure: \( P = P_0 + \rho g h \)

Force on Submerged Plane: \( F = \gamma h_c A \), \( h_c = \text{centroid depth} \)

Center of Pressure: \( y_p = y_c + \frac{I_c}{A y_c} \)

Buoyancy (Archimedes): \( F_B = \rho g V_{\text{displaced}} \)

3. Fluid Kinematics

Velocity Field: \( \vec{v} = u \hat{i} + v \hat{j} + w \hat{k} \)

Acceleration: \( \vec{a} = \frac{D\vec{v}}{Dt} = \frac{\partial \vec{v}}{\partial t} + (\vec{v} \cdot \nabla) \vec{v} \)

Stream Function (2D Incompressible): \( u = \frac{\partial \psi}{\partial y} \), \( v = -\frac{\partial \psi}{\partial x} \)

Continuity Equation: \( \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot (\rho \vec{v}) = 0 \)

Incompressible Flow: \( \nabla \cdot \vec{v} = 0 \)

4. Fluid Dynamics (Momentum)

Navier-Stokes Equations (Incompressible): \( \rho \left( \frac{\partial \vec{v}}{\partial t} + (\vec{v} \cdot \nabla) \vec{v} \right) = -\nabla P + \mu \nabla^2 \vec{v} + \rho \vec{g} \)

Momentum Equation (Control Volume): \( \sum \vec{F} = \frac{d}{dt} \int_{\text{CV}} \rho \vec{v} \, dV + \int_{\text{CS}} \rho \vec{v} (\vec{v} \cdot \hat{n}) \, dA \)

Bernoulli Equation (Steady, Incompressible, Inviscid): \( P + \frac{1}{2} \rho v^2 + \rho g h = \text{constant} \)

Euler’s Equation (Inviscid): \( \rho \frac{D \vec{v}}{Dt} = -\nabla P + \rho \vec{g} \)

5. Energy Equation

Energy Equation (Control Volume): \( \dot{Q} - \dot{W} = \dot{m} \left[ \left( h_2 - h_1 \right) + \frac{1}{2} (v_2^2 - v_1^2) + g (z_2 - z_1) \right] \)

Head Form (Bernoulli with Losses): \( \frac{P_1}{\gamma} + \frac{v_1^2}{2g} + z_1 = \frac{P_2}{\gamma} + \frac{v_2^2}{2g} + z_2 + h_L \)

Head Loss: \( h_L = f \frac{L}{D} \frac{v^2}{2g} \) (Darcy-Weisbach)

6. Flow in Pipes

Reynolds Number: \( \text{Re} = \frac{\rho v D}{\mu} = \frac{v D}{\nu} \)

Friction Factor (Laminar): \( f = \frac{64}{\text{Re}} \)

Friction Factor (Turbulent, Colebrook): \( \frac{1}{\sqrt{f}} = -2 \log_{10} \left( \frac{\epsilon/D}{3.7} + \frac{2.51}{\text{Re} \sqrt{f}} \right) \)

Minor Losses: \( h_{L,\text{minor}} = K \frac{v^2}{2g} \), \( K = \text{loss coefficient} \)

Pipe Flow Rate: \( Q = v A \)

7. Dimensional Analysis and Similarity

Drag Force: \( F_D = \frac{1}{2} \rho v^2 C_D A \)

Lift Force: \( F_L = \frac{1}{2} \rho v^2 C_L A \)

Drag Coefficient: \( C_D = f(\text{Re}, \text{shape}) \)

Weber Number: \( \text{We} = \frac{\rho v^2 L}{\sigma} \)

Froude Number: \( \text{Fr} = \frac{v}{\sqrt{g L}} \)

Mach Number: \( \text{Ma} = \frac{v}{c} \), \( c = \text{speed of sound} \)

8. Open Channel Flow

Manning’s Equation: \( v = \frac{1}{n} R_h^{2/3} S^{1/2} \), \( R_h = \frac{A}{P} \)

Froude Number: \( \text{Fr} = \frac{v}{\sqrt{g y}} \), \( y = \text{flow depth} \)

Specific Energy: \( E = y + \frac{v^2}{2g} \)

Critical Depth: \( \text{Fr} = 1 \), \( v = \sqrt{g y_c} \)

9. Compressible Flow

Speed of Sound: \( c = \sqrt{\gamma R T} \), \( \gamma = \frac{c_p}{c_v} \)

Isentropic Flow Relations: \( \frac{P_1}{P_2} = \left( \frac{T_1}{T_2} \right)^{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma-1}} \)

Stagnation Pressure: \( P_0 = P \left( 1 + \frac{\gamma-1}{2} \text{Ma}^2 \right)^{\frac{\gamma}{\gamma-1}} \)

Mass Flow Rate: \( \dot{m} = \rho A v \)

10. Boundary Layer and Flow Separation

Boundary Layer Thickness: \( \delta \approx \frac{5x}{\sqrt{\text{Re}_x}} \) (laminar flat plate)

Displacement Thickness: \( \delta^* = \int_0^\delta \left( 1 - \frac{u}{U_\infty} \right) dy \)

Shear Stress: \( \tau_w = \mu \left( \frac{\partial u}{\partial y} \right)_{y=0} \)

Drag Coefficient (Flat Plate, Laminar): \( C_D = \frac{1.328}{\sqrt{\text{Re}_L}} \)
